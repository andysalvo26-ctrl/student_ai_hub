# Boundaries: Non-Negotiable Constraints

## Content Constraints

### Descriptive, Not Instructional
- Describe what is, not what should be
- Avoid prescriptive language ("students should," "you must")
- No recommendations or advice to students
- No "best practices" or guidance

### No Tools, Vendors, or Recommendations
- Do not mention specific AI tools, platforms, or vendors
- Refer generically: "AI tools," "AI systems," "AI applications"
- Do not provide recommendations about tool selection or use

### No Claims Beyond Canon
- All factual claims must be traceable to canonical materials
- Do not invent statistics, facts, or claims
- If information is missing, note limitation rather than inventing
- Reference PackA for AI concepts, this MAJOR_CANON.md for major description

### No Advice to Students
- Do not provide instructional guidance
- Do not tell students what to do
- Describe contexts and capabilities, not actions students should take

### No Final Conclusions Without Human Approval
- All drafts remain marked as DRAFT until human approval
- Do not mark content as "final" or "complete"
- Human author must explicitly approve before proceeding
- Human rewriting is required before any text is frozen

## Tone Constraints

- Institutional, descriptive tone
- Neutral language appropriate for Smeal College of Business website
- Avoid prescriptive or instructional language
- Focus on description rather than guidance

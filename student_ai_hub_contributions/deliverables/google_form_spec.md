# AI News That Matters — Google Form (Simplified)

Build this form in Google Forms. Takes ~10 minutes.

---

## Form Title

**AI News That Matters — Submit an Entry**

## Form Description

```
Submit an entry for the "AI News That Matters" section of the Student AI Hub.

Before submitting, read the contribution guide (PDF) to understand the expected format and tone. You can also upload the guide to ChatGPT or another AI assistant to help with drafting.

All submissions are reviewed by editors before publication.
```

---

## Section 1: About You

### 1. Name
- **Type:** Short answer
- **Required:** Yes

### 2. Penn State Email
- **Type:** Short answer
- **Required:** Yes
- **Validation:** Response must contain "@psu.edu"

### 3. Year
- **Type:** Dropdown
- **Required:** Yes
- **Options:**
  - Freshman
  - Sophomore
  - Junior
  - Senior
  - Graduate Student
  - Faculty / Staff

### 4. Are you an Applied AI Club member?
- **Type:** Multiple choice
- **Required:** Yes
- **Options:**
  - Yes
  - No
  - Not sure

---

## Section 2: Your Source Article

### 5. Article URL
- **Type:** Short answer
- **Required:** Yes
- **Description:** Link to the article you're summarizing

### 6. Publication Name
- **Type:** Short answer
- **Required:** Yes
- **Description:** e.g., AP News, The Verge, MIT Technology Review

### 7. Article Date
- **Type:** Date
- **Required:** Yes

---

## Section 3: Your Entry

### 8. Headline
- **Type:** Short answer
- **Required:** Yes
- **Description:** A short, descriptive headline (no opinions or hype)

### 9. What Happened
- **Type:** Paragraph
- **Required:** Yes
- **Description:** 2–4 sentences summarizing the facts. What was announced, released, or reported?

### 10. Why It Matters to Students
- **Type:** Paragraph
- **Required:** Yes
- **Description:** 2–3 sentences. Why would a Penn State student care about this?

### 11. What to Watch Next
- **Type:** Paragraph
- **Required:** Yes
- **Description:** 1–2 sentences. What's still uncertain or might change?

---

## Section 4: Credit & Permissions

### 12. How would you like to be credited?
- **Type:** Multiple choice
- **Required:** Yes
- **Options:**
  - Use my full name
  - Use my first name and last initial (e.g., "John S.")
  - Credit as "Anonymous"
  - Credit as "Applied AI Club Member"

### 13. Permission to publish
- **Type:** Checkbox
- **Required:** Yes
- **Label:** I give permission for this entry to be published on the Student AI Hub (with light editing if needed)

### 14. Anything else?
- **Type:** Paragraph
- **Required:** No
- **Description:** Optional notes for editors

---

## Form Settings

| Setting | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Collect email addresses | Yes (automatically via Google) |
| Limit to 1 response | No |
| Confirmation message | "Thanks for your submission! Our editors will review it and may reach out if we have questions." |
| Response destination | Create a new Google Sheet |

---

## After Building the Form

1. **Link the PDF guide** — Add a link to the contribution guide PDF in the form description
2. **Share with editors** — Give your editor team access to the linked Google Sheet
3. **Test it** — Submit a test entry and verify it appears in the Sheet
4. **Distribute** — Share the form link with @psu.edu audiences

---

## How Editors Use Responses

1. Open the linked Google Sheet
2. Review new submissions (each row = one submission)
3. For any submission, you can:
   - **Copy the row** into a doc for drafting
   - **Export as CSV** and use with AI tools
   - **Download as PDF** (via Google Sheets → File → Download)
4. Draft the final post manually or with AI assistance
5. (Optional) Send preview back to contributor via email
6. Publish to Wix blog
